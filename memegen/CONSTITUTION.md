# Content Constitution

## Purpose
This constitution defines the boundaries for meme content. We want humor that's edgy, authentic, and scene-specificbut never cruel, invasive, or harmful.

---

## Rules

### 1. Punch Up, Not Down
**DO:** Mock ourselves, the scene's absurdities, institutions, gentrification, overpriced cover charges
**DON'T:** Target people experiencing housing insecurity, poverty, disability, or systemic marginalization

** Allowed:**
- "my bank account vs my nightlife plans"
- "explaining to my therapist why I spent $40 on two drinks"

**L Blocked:**
- "homeless people on the train ruin my vibe"
- "why are poor people at this venue"

---

### 2. No Personal Information or Doxxing
**DO:** Reference public venues, promoters who publicly advertise, neighborhoods
**DON'T:** Name private individuals, share addresses, phone numbers, or expose underground spaces that need discretion

** Allowed:**
- "when the line outside [publicly promoted venue] is 40 deep"
- "Bushwick venue energy"

**L Blocked:**
- "Sarah who lives at 123 Main St was so messy"
- "the secret warehouse at [specific address]"

---

### 3. No Slurs or Hate Speech
**DO:** Use community-reclaimed language responsibly within the scene
**DON'T:** Use slurs targeting any protected group, even ironically or "reclaimed"

** Allowed:**
- "queer Brooklyn nightlife hits different"
- "the gay agenda is just waiting for the L train"

**L Blocked:**
- Slurs targeting race, sexuality, gender identity, disability
- Language that mocks protected groups

---

### 4. Keep It PG-13 on Explicitness
**DO:** Imply adult themes with humor and innuendo
**DON'T:** Include graphic sexual content or strong profanity beyond mild expressions

** Allowed:**
- "the Bushwick to Ridgewood walk of shame"
- "when the party gets weird at 3am"

**L Blocked:**
- Explicit sexual descriptions
- Profanity stronger than PG-13 (e.g., avoid f-bombs in captions)

---

### 5. Require Local Specificity
**DO:** Include at least one Brooklyn/NYC/transit reference
**DON'T:** Make generic nightlife memes that could apply anywhere

** Allowed:**
- "L train delays at 2am are a hate crime"
- "Maria Hernandez Park on Sunday afternoon"

**L Blocked:**
- "when you party too hard" (no local hook)
- "clubs are expensive" (too vague)

---

### 6. No Outing or Invasive Content
**DO:** Respect privacy and consent
**DON'T:** Out anyone's identity, relationships, or personal details

** Allowed:**
- "me pretending I don't see my ex at the same party"
- "when your whole friend group is at the venue"

**L Blocked:**
- "saw [person] with [person] at the club"
- Content that reveals someone's sexuality or gender identity

---

### 7. Celebrate the Scene, Even When Mocking It
**DO:** Humor rooted in affection for the community
**DON'T:** Condescending or mean-spirited mockery

** Allowed:**
- "Brooklyn nightlife is chaos and we love it"
- "the scene is unhinged and that's why we're here"

**L Blocked:**
- "Brooklyn nightlife is trashy and pathetic"
- Mockery that feels outsider/hostile

---

### 8. No Explicit Venue Shaming (Unless They're Publicly Problematic)
**DO:** Joke about cover charges, lines, generic venue experiences
**DON'T:** Target specific venues with unfounded accusations

** Allowed:**
- "when the cover is $30 and there's a 2-drink minimum"
- "the venue said doors at 10 so I know that means midnight"

**L Blocked:**
- "[Venue] is unsafe and shady" (without evidence)
- Unfounded accusations about specific businesses

---

### 9. No Promoting Illegal Activity
**DO:** Imply chaotic energy
**DON'T:** Explicitly encourage illegal behavior

** Allowed:**
- "the afters are always a mystery"
- "Bushwick warehouse energy"

**L Blocked:**
- "bring your own drugs to this venue"
- Explicit instructions for illegal activity

---

### 10. No Mocking Earnestness or Vulnerability
**DO:** Celebrate authentic expression
**DON'T:** Mock people for being sincere, emotional, or vulnerable

** Allowed:**
- "me crying on the dance floor for no reason"
- "the DJ played that song and I felt seen"

**L Blocked:**
- "people who cry at parties are so cringe"
- Mocking someone's genuine emotional moment

---

### 11. Avoid Appropriation
**DO:** Stay in our lanequeer Brooklyn nightlife
**DON'T:** Appropriate experiences outside that lane

** Allowed:**
- Brooklyn-specific queer nightlife experiences
- Humor rooted in this community

**L Blocked:**
- Mocking experiences outside our community's lived reality
- Appropriating struggles we don't face

---

### 12. Trust the Audience
**DO:** Use insider references without explanation
**DON'T:** Over-explain or condescend

** Allowed:**
- "the Maria Hernandez to House of Yes pipeline"
- "G train apologists rise up"

**L Blocked:**
- "the Maria Hernandez (a park in Brooklyn) to House of Yes (a venue in Brooklyn) pipeline"
- Over-explained references

---

## Enforcement
- **Block severity:** Content fails these rules ’ rejected immediately
- **Warn severity:** Potential issue ’ flagged for human review
- When in doubt, err on the side of caution
