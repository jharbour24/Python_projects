# Meme Style Guide

## Audience

Queer folks ages 20–40 who go out in Brooklyn. Culturally literate about nightlife, transit, and neighborhood dynamics.

## Core Principles

### DO

1. **Punch up at systems, not down at people**
   - ✅ "the MTA sees your club outfit at 2 PM and knows"
   - ❌ "tourists in Times Square looking lost"

2. **Use local specificity**
   - ✅ "taking the L from Bedford to 1st Ave: a spiritual journey"
   - ❌ "taking the subway is annoying"

3. **Self-deprecating humor**
   - ✅ "me: I'll leave at 10. also me: texting 'omw' from bed at 11:30"
   - ❌ "people who arrive late are the worst"

4. **Affectionate insider jokes**
   - ✅ "Brooklyn venue dress code: no sneakers (everyone wearing sneakers)"
   - ❌ "dress codes are stupid"

5. **Deadpan/understated delivery**
   - ✅ "the G train is conceptual"
   - ❌ "THE G TRAIN NEVER COMES OMG!!!"

### DON'T

1. **No punching down**
   - ❌ Targeting individuals, body shaming, or mocking marginalized groups

2. **No doxxing**
   - ❌ Specific venue names without permission
   - ✅ "a venue in Bushwick" or "somewhere off the JMZ"

3. **No outing people**
   - ❌ References to specific people's identities or personal lives

4. **No strong profanity**
   - ✅ PG-13 language ("hell," "damn")
   - ❌ R-rated language

5. **No hashtags or spam**
   - ❌ "#brooklyn #nightlife #queer"

## Tone Options

### Dry
Deadpan, matter-of-fact, minimal emotion.

**Example:**
> "arriving on time to a Brooklyn party: a mistake"

### Slightly Unhinged
Chaotic energy, maybe capitals, extra punctuation.

**Example:**
> "texting 'five minutes away' from a train that hasn't arrived yet"

### Coy
Playful, knowing wink, shared secret.

**Example:**
> "oh you NEED directions? how nice for you"

## Local Hooks (Required)

Every meme must reference at least one:

- **Boroughs/Neighborhoods**: Brooklyn, Bushwick, Bed-Stuy, Williamsburg, Ridgewood
- **Transit**: L train, G train, JMZ, MTA delays
- **Venue Culture**: cover charges, lines, dress codes, cash-only bars
- **Seasonal**: coat check season, summer rooftops, February doldrums

## Template Guidance

### Drake (reject/prefer)
**Best for:** Comparing obvious choice vs. actual behavior

**Example:**
> Top: taking the express train
> Bottom: following people in good outfits through an industrial area

### Tweet Screenshot
**Best for:** One-liner observations

**Example:**
> "the L train at 2 AM on Saturday is a fashion show, a therapy session, and a hostage situation"

### Two-Panel (before/after)
**Best for:** Expectation vs. reality

**Example:**
> Before: "I'll leave at 10"
> After: texting "omw" at midnight from bed

### Distracted Boyfriend
**Best for:** Temptation/distraction dynamics

**Example:**
> Boyfriend: my bed
> Girlfriend: work tomorrow
> Other girl: Bushwick at 1 AM on a Tuesday

### Top Text / Bottom Text
**Best for:** Classic observational humor

**Example:**
> BROOKLYN NIGHTLIFE
> paying $20 cover to stand in a warehouse

## Devices

### Exaggeration
"Getting to a Ridgewood venue requires 3 trains, a prayer, and directions from someone who's already lost"

### Misdirection
"Brooklyn venue: no sneakers. Also Brooklyn venue: literally everyone in sneakers"

### Code-Switch
Mix high/low registers: "taking the MTA: a pedestrian commute or a Homeric odyssey?"

### Deadpan Specificity
"cover charge: $10. cover charge after you're in line: $25. cover charge after you paid: $10 again somehow"

## Caption Rules

- **Maximum 14 words** (strict)
- **No hashtags**
- **No @mentions**
- **Readable at small size**
- **Clear without image** (caption should make sense alone)

## Examples (Do/Don't Pairs)

### Example 1: Transit

✅ **DO:** "the L train sees you in club attire at 3 PM and simply knows"

❌ **DON'T:** "people who ride the subway in weird clothes LOL"

**Why:** First punches up (MTA as observer), second punches down at individuals.

### Example 2: Venue Culture

✅ **DO:** "Brooklyn dress code: creative black tie (denim)"

❌ **DON'T:** "everyone at [Venue Name] looks ridiculous"

**Why:** First is affectionate self-deprecation, second targets a real place.

### Example 3: Local Specificity

✅ **DO:** "taking the JMZ: a test of faith and patience"

❌ **DON'T:** "public transportation is bad"

**Why:** First is specific to Brooklyn transit, second is generic.

### Example 4: Tone

✅ **DO (dry):** "arriving punctually to a Brooklyn function: gauche"

✅ **DO (unhinged):** "TEXTING 'HERE' WHILE STILL ON THE TRAIN"

❌ **DON'T:** "ppl who show up on time r annoying af"

**Why:** First two use humor devices, last is just complaining.

- [ ] No profanity stronger than PG-13
- [ ] No real venue/people names
- [ ] Tone matches device
- [ ] Makes sense without image
