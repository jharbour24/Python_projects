# Meme Style Guide

## Audience
Queer folks (ages 20-40) who go out in Brooklyn nightlife. Knows the venues, the lines, the trains, the chaos.

## Core Principles

###  DO
1. **Punch up, self-deprecate**  Make fun of ourselves, the scene, the absurdity
2. **Hyper-local specificity**  Name trains, neighborhoods, venues (when publicly promoting)
3. **Affectionate mockery**  We love this scene; humor comes from love
4. **Current moments**  Reference recent venue changes, transit drama, seasonal vibes
5. **Insider vocabulary**  Use scene-specific slang naturally (not forced)
6. **Celebrate chaos**  The scene is messy and that's part of the charm

### L DON'T
1. **Punch down**  No mocking vulnerable groups, housing insecurity, etc.
2. **Name individuals**  Unless they're public figures/promoters with consent
3. **Venue doxxing**  Don't expose underground/DIY spaces that need discretion
4. **Sanitize**  Don't make it corporate-friendly; keep the edge
5. **Explain**  Trust the audience knows the references
6. **Force diversity**  If it's not authentic, skip it

## Micro-Examples

###  GOOD
**Caption:** "when you say you're leaving at midnight but the L train has other plans"
**Why:** Self-deprecating, hyper-local (L train), relatable nightlife moment

**Caption:** "POV: you're the only sober person at the Bushwick afters"
**Why:** Affectionate scene observation, specific neighborhood reference

**Caption:** "the Maria Hernandez to House of Yes pipeline is real"
**Why:** Insider reference to neighborhood progression, playful tone

**Caption:** "me explaining why the G train is actually good"
**Why:** Self-aware defense of something universally mocked

**Caption:** "telling myself this is the last Ridgewood party narrator: it was not"
**Why:** Self-deprecating, neighborhood-specific, recognizable pattern

### L BAD
**Caption:** "when you party too hard and regret it"
**Why:** Too generic, no local hook, could be anywhere

**Caption:** "Sarah at [venue name] was so messy last night"
**Why:** Names an individual, potentially invasive

**Caption:** "homeless people on the train are so annoying"
**Why:** Punches down at vulnerable group

**Caption:** "when the bougie Manhattan people invade Bushwick"
**Why:** Class-shaming that feels mean-spirited, not playful

**Caption:** "this meme is about the LGBTQIA+ community in Brooklyn, New York"
**Why:** Over-explained, corporate tone, no humor

## Tone Spectrum

**Dry**  Deadpan, matter-of-fact observation
*Example:* "the L train to therapy pipeline"

**Slightly unhinged**  Embracing the chaos, mild absurdity
*Example:* "me at 3am trying to explain why Ridgewood is technically Brooklyn"

**Coy**  Playful, knowing wink
*Example:* "nobody: / me: time to check if [venue] posted the lineup yet"

---

## Template Fit

**Drake / Distracted Boyfriend**  Comparing two choices (usually one good, one bad)
*Example:* Drake pushing away "leaving at midnight" / embracing "staying til 4am"

**Tweet Screenshot**  Observational humor, hot takes, relatable moments
*Example:* "the JMZ being down is a cultural reset we didn't ask for"

**Two-Panel**  Before/after, expectation/reality
*Example:* Panel 1: "me planning to have one drink" / Panel 2: "me at 2am"

**Top Text / Bottom Text**  Classic meme format for broad observations
*Example:* Top: "TELLING MYSELF I'LL LEAVE EARLY" / Bottom: "NARRATOR: SHE DID NOT"

---

## Local Reference Bank
- **Trains:** L, G, JMZ, M, A/C at Nostrand
- **Neighborhoods:** Bushwick, Ridgewood, Bed-Stuy, Crown Heights, Williamsburg (with irony)
- **Parks:** Maria Hernandez, Prospect Park, McCarren
- **Venues:** Use only if publicly promoting; otherwise use "the venue" or "the spot"
- **Vibes:** DIY, pop-up, warehouse, afters, day party, drag brunch

---

## Word Budget: d14 Words
Captions must be punchy. If you need more, you're explaining too much.

**Too long:** "when you tell yourself you're going to leave the party early but then your favorite song comes on and you end up staying"
**Better:** "telling myself I'll leave early knowing that's a lie"
